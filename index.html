Stop Grasping For Minnows; Reel In The Big Fish With Next Level SEO

Want to know why your current SEO strategy isn’t getting results? Find out how to take your search engine optimization to the next level in three easy steps.

Modern consumers are smart. Why shouldn’t your SEO be smart too?

With only the most basic research, anyone can quickly churn out pages of content stuffed with SEO keywords chosen to target your audience’s searches.

This may have been an effective strategy in the past, but with the rapidly growing complexity of internet search algorithms, this is no longer enough to keep your material above water.

The ocean of content that consumers are sifting through with their search queries is vast. No matter how novel and attention-catching your idea is, the market is likely already over-saturated with keyword-drenched fluff.

Here is an easy way to think about it; keywords are just the bait.

Picture this: you are bobbing in a small boat in the middle of an immense lake filled with tasty fish of all shapes and sizes. You have brought with you a container of the most plump, juicy worms that money can buy. Now imagine if you were to suddenly dump the entire container overboard with nothing attached to any of the worms.

The thought of fishing in this way seems ridiculous! And yet people treat keywords in this manner every day.

Don’t get me wrong: keywords are an essential tool for drawing in traffic.

However, writing quick, meaningless content filled with trendy search terms and tossing it onto your website or blog is the equivalent of dumping a can of worms over the side of your boat and expecting the fish to magically appear in your hands. In a best-case-scenario, perhaps you could reach into the water and grab some small fish that were attracted by the worms.

But you don’t want minnows. You want to catch the big fish.

To do this, you need bait, yes. But you also need a hook to catch them, a line to hold them, and a reel to draw them in. This is where the real engine optimization comes in. The best SEO content includes all of these tools working together in harmony, not just the bait. Finding strong keywords an art, but it is only half of the battle.

How can we use this knowledge to our advantage?

These three steps are what separate amateurs from SEO experts:

1) The Placement of Keywords

This is a big one. Not all locations weigh equally in the search engine formula. You have probably thought about including keywords in your page title, but have you tried including them in:

  • Various subheadings
  • Page’s URL
  • First paragraph
  • Last paragraph
  • Meta descriptions and metadata such as image titles, alt text, and file names
  • Anchor text for links

2) Strategic SEO Variations

Don’t use the exact same keyword or key phrase each time. It is clunky, off-putting, and limits your possibilities. Try variations! This can mean:

  • Plurals
  • Playing with word order
  • Synonyms
  • Creative phrasing
Did you get all that? Now let’s take it up a notch.

3) What’s The Real Point of SEO? Actions!

Here is where we separate the meek from the mighty. Take a moment to think about why you are using SEO. To draw traffic to your website or content, right? But why? What is the real goal of bringing people to your site?

To put it simply, what are the actions that you want people to take once they get to your website? Do you want them to:

  • Purchase something?
  • Click to create ad revenue?
  • Subscribe to your mailing list?
  • Spread the word on social media?
  • Get hooked by your content enough to visit additional pages of your site?
  • Perceive you as a go-to authority on your niche?
Take a moment to decide which actions best fit your goals.

The first step in a successful SEO strategy is knowing your desired end result. The best SEO techniques for you will differ depending on your target actions, but consider a few of the following ideas:

  • Subtly bold, highlight, or italicize a high traffic keyword to draw attention to an important part of your page (subscribe button, call-to-action, advertisements, etc.). People will be scanning the page for the keywords that brought them to your site. Take advantage of this.
  • Remember the subheadings tip from above? Sprinkling SEO keywords in your subheadings also serves the purpose of assuring viewers that your page is relevant to their search and keeps them engaged. Win/win!
  • Take advantage of social media to win an edge over the competition. Using strategically placed keywords, attract attention to built in links to Twitter, Instagram, or Facebook. Gently pepper your social media updates and bios with important words and phrases. Even if they love your website, people don’t want to work hard to spread the word. Make it easy for them!
  • Include images in social media messages containing your keywords. People are significantly more likely to share content that includes images.
One of the most important caveats to the above keyword advice is not to overdo it. In this situation, it is absolutely possible to have too much of a good thing.

If you have keywords packed too densely into your content, it will be obvious to the reader, and potentially even to Google’s algorithm, which could hurt your ranking on search engine results pages (SERPs).
